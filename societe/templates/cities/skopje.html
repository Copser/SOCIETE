HISTORY

 

The history of New York begins around 10,000 BC, when the first Native Americans arrive. By
1100 AD, New York's main native cultures, the Iroquian and Algonquian, had developed.
European discovery of New York was led by the French in 1524 and the first land claim came in
1609 by the Dutch. As part of New Netherland, the colony was important in the fur trade and
eventually became an agricultural recourse thanks to the patroon system. In 1626 the Dutch
bought the island of Manhattan from Native Americans. In 1664, England renamed the colony
New York, after the Duke of York. New York City gained prominence in the 18th century as a
major trading port in the Thirteen Colonies. The first native New Yorkers were the Lenape, an
Algonquin people who hunted, fished and farmed in the area between the Delawere and Hudson
rivers. The first European was Giovanni da Verrazzano, and Italian who sailed up and down the
Atlantic coas in search of a route to Asia-but none setteled there unitl 1624.
